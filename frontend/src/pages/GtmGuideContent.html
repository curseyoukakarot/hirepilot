What this playbook actually does

This isn’t another GTM theory doc or collection of tactics.

This playbook shows you how to turn HirePilot into your GTM operating system — even if you’ve never set up automations, never used Zapier, and have only ever run pipeline out of spreadsheets, DMs, and Google Sheets.

By the end of this playbook, you’ll have:

  • Plug-and-play outbound sequences already mapped to personas, campaigns, and dashboards in HirePilot.
  • Step-by-step workflows that connect RB2B, Apollo, Stripe, Calendly, and your email into one simple system.
  • Dashboards that tell you what’s working, what’s not, and where to focus tomorrow — without needing a data team.
  • A practical automation map so you know exactly what to automate and what to keep human, with safety and deliverability as non-negotiables.

You can follow this playbook line-by-line and build everything in a weekend, or implement one recipe per week until the full system is live.

Who this is for
Founders, recruiters, agencies, and fractional leaders

…who want predictable pipeline without the chaos of spreadsheets and scattered tools.

Who this is NOT for
Teams who like manual processes

…or don’t want to measure what works.

Section 1: Plug and Play Sequences

Starting with Section 1 (Plug-and-Play Sequences). Below is “recipe-grade” detail, written for someone who has never built sequences before.

1. Plug-and-Play Sequences

1.1 What sequences are in HirePilot

A sequence is a pre-written series of touches (emails, LinkedIn steps, tasks) that HirePilot sends or schedules for you so you don’t have to remember who to follow up with each day.

In this playbook, each sequence will have:

  • A trigger (what puts someone into the sequence).
  • The touches (emails, LinkedIn, calls) with timing.
  • The build steps (how to set it up in HirePilot + where Zapier comes in, if needed).

1.2 Funding Signal Sequence

Goal
Turn funding news (or similar GTM trigger) into predictable conversations and meetings.
A. When to use this
  • Your ideal buyers just raised a round (Seed–Series C), changed GTM leadership, or announced an aggressive hiring/expansion plan.
  • You want to offer: GTM hiring support, recruiting as a service, or a GTM pipeline audit.
B. Sequence structure (touches)

Timing is flexible, but a good starting point:

  • Day 1 – Email 1: Congrats + short, specific POV on GTM at their stage.
  • Day 3 – Email 2: “3 mistakes companies at [Stage] make after raising” + quick diagnostic.
  • Day 5 – LinkedIn: Connect with a short note (no long pitch).
  • Day 7 – Email 3: Offer a 20–30 minute GTM hiring or pipeline gap audit.
  • Day 10 – Task: Call or voice note if there was any engagement (opens/replies/clicks).
C. Fields you need in HirePilot

Make sure your Leads and Companies tables have:

  • Company Name
  • Contact Name
  • Email
  • LinkedIn URL (if you can get it)
  • Stage (Seed, Series A, Series B, etc.)
  • Funding Date / Trigger Date
  • Persona (e.g., “Seed–Series A CEO”, “VP Sales”, “RevOps Leader”)
  • Source (e.g., “Funding List”, “Apollo Funding Search”)
D. How to build this in HirePilot (step-by-step)
Create a Campaign
  • Go to Campaigns → New Campaign.
  • Name: “Funding Signal – [Persona]”.
  • Type: Outbound.
  • Attach Persona: choose the persona this is for (e.g., Seed–Series A CEO).
Add emails to the sequence
  • Inside the campaign, open “Email Sequence” or “Steps”.
  • Step 1: Email – Day 1.
  • Subject example: “Congrats on the raise – quick GTM lens?”
  • Body: Use your playbook copy (keep it short, 3–5 sentences).
  • Send delay: “Send immediately when contact is added to campaign”.
  • Step 2: Email – Day 3. Delay: “3 days after previous email”.
  • Subject example: “3 GTM hires Seed/Series A teams get wrong”.
  • Step 3: LinkedIn Task – Day 5. Type: Task (LinkedIn connect). Delay: “2 days after previous email”.
  • Step 4: Email – Day 7. Delay: “2 days after LinkedIn Task is due”. CTA: “20-minute GTM gap audit”.
  • Step 5: Call Task – Day 10. Type: Task (Call). Delay: “3 days after last email”.
Define enrollment rules (who gets added)
  • In the campaign, go to “Enrollment / Targeting”.
  • Filter Leads where:
  • Stage = your chosen stages (Seed/Series A/Series B).
  • Funding Date is within last 30–45 days.
  • Persona matches.
  • Save as “Funding Signal – [Stage] Saved View”.
Turn on throttles for safety
  • Set daily send limit per user (e.g., max 50–75 new sends/day).
  • Set working hours (e.g., 8:30 am – 11:30 am recipient local time).
  • Make sure “Skip leads contacted in last X days” is ON (e.g., 30–90 days).
E. If you have zero Zapier experience: simple automation version

You can start without Zapier:

  • Once per week, import a CSV from Apollo (Funding search) into HirePilot’s Leads table.
  • Tag those Leads with Source = “Funding – [Month]”.
  • In the Campaign, go to “Add from view” and choose your “Funding Signal – [Stage] Saved View”.
  • Start the campaign at low volume (10–20/day), then increase.
  • Zapier only becomes necessary once you want this fully always-on, which we’ll cover in the Workflows section.

1.3 Event Bridge Sequence

Goal
Turn events (webinars, workshops, roundtables) into meetings, not just registrations.
A. When to use this
  • You run a webinar or event that attracts your ICP (e.g., “2026 GTM Hiring Predictions”).
  • You want more meetings and pipeline from each event, not just “attendance”.
B. Sequence structure

Use a simple pattern that matches your GTM guide:

  • Day -7: Email 1 – Invite to the event.
  • Day 0 (morning): Email 2 – Reminder + what they’ll get.
  • Day +1: Email 3 – Replay + “Here’s what stood out for teams like yours” + soft CTA.
  • Day +3–4: Email 4 – “Want a custom scorecard or 1:1 breakdown?” clear CTA to book.

Optionally add:

  • LinkedIn connect after registration.
  • Task to personalize outreach for high-intent registrants.
C. Fields you need

In HirePilot, ensure:

  • Events table (Name, Date, Type, Topic).
  • Leads table has: Registered (Yes/No), Attended (Yes/No), No-show (Yes/No).
  • Source = Event Name.
D. Build the sequence in HirePilot
Create an Event Campaign
  • Campaign Name: “Event Bridge – [Event Name]”.
  • Type: Event / Nurture.
Add steps
  • Step 1: Email – Invite (Day -7). You can send this manually or as a separate “Prospects to invite” campaign.
  • Step 2: Email – Reminder (Day 0, a few hours before start). Delay: “On event date at [time]” or “Same day at 8:30am”.
  • Step 3: Email – Replay (Day +1). Delay: “1 day after event date”.
  • Step 4: Email – Custom scorecard / audit CTA (Day +3–4). Delay: “3 days after event date”.
Segment by behavior (optional but powerful)
  • Create views: “Registered & Attended”, “Registered, No-show”, “Clicked replay link”.
  • You can clone the sequence and tweak copy for each segment later.
E. No-code workflow version (no Zapier yet)

If you’re not ready for automation:

  • Export registrants from Zoom/Event tool as CSV.
  • Import into HirePilot as Leads (map to Event fields).
  • Manually add them to the “Event Bridge – [Event]” campaign.
  • Later, automation will handle this via Zapier/Make.

1.4 Inbound Intelligence Sequence (RB2B → HirePilot)

Goal
Turn anonymous website visitors into warm conversations using RB2B + HirePilot.
A. When to use this
  • You have RB2B set up to see which companies visit your website.
  • You want to outreach only to relevant, high-intent visits (pricing, GTM guide, demo).
B. Sequence structure
  • Day 1 – Email 1: Soft “noticed [Company] was exploring GTM / hiring” email, highly contextual.
  • Day 2–3 – LinkedIn connect: Short, non-pushy note.
  • Day 4–5 – Email 2: Offer a quick audit/scorecard or “happy to walk you through how teams like yours use HirePilot”.
  • Day 7–10 – Task: Call or Loom if there was any engagement.
C. Fields you need in HirePilot

For Leads / Companies:

  • Company Name (from RB2B).
  • Website URL.
  • Page Category visited (pricing, GTM guide, demo, careers, etc.).
  • Visit Intensity (pageviews or “score” if RB2B provides it).
  • Persona (once you add the right contact).
  • Source = “RB2B Inbound”.
D. Simple manual version (no automation yet)

Until workflows are set up:

  • RB2B posts leads into Slack.
  • Once per day, copy the good-fit ones into a CSV or directly into HirePilot Leads.
  • Tag them with Source = RB2B and Page Category.
  • Add them to the “Inbound Intelligence” campaign with lower daily volume (e.g., 5–10/day).
  • In the Workflow section, this will become your showcase RB2B → HirePilot → outreach automation.

1.5 ABM Multi-Touch Sequence

Goal
Land must-win accounts with orchestrated multi-channel touches.
A. When to use this
  • You have a list of 25–100 “dream” accounts.
  • You’re willing to put more effort per account (research, personalization).
B. Sequence structure (from your GTM guide)

Example 8-touch template:

  • Day 1: LinkedIn connect.
  • Day 2: Email 1 (trigger-based, very specific).
  • Day 3: LinkedIn comment + like on a relevant post.
  • Day 5: Call (only if engaged/opened).
  • Day 6: Share asset (clip/playbook relevant to their situation).
  • Day 8: Email 2 (case study focused).
  • Day 10: Event invite or curated resource.
  • Day 12: Call #2 + ask directly for meeting.
C. ABM structure in HirePilot

Create:

  • ABM Accounts table — Fields: Account Name, Tier (1–3), Industry, Size, ICP fit, Status (Not started / In progress / Won / Lost).
  • Stakeholders table (or use Leads with an Account link) — Fields: Name, Role, Seniority, Email, LinkedIn URL, Buying role (Champion, Decision-maker, etc.).
D. Build the ABM sequence in HirePilot
Create a Campaign
  • Name: “ABM – Tier 1 Multi-Touch”.
  • Type: ABM.
Add steps (mostly Tasks + optional email templates)
  • Step 1: Task – Send LinkedIn connect (Day 1).
  • Step 2: Email – Day 2 (with personalization tokens).
  • Step 3: Task – Engage with LinkedIn posts (Day 3).
  • Step 4: Task – Call (Day 5).
  • Step 5: Task or Email – Share asset (Day 6).
  • Step 6: Email – Case study (Day 8).
  • Step 7: Email or Task – Event invite (Day 10).
  • Step 8: Task – Second call (Day 12).
Assign ownership
  • Ensure each Account has an Owner in HirePilot.
  • Set Tasks to assign to that Owner by default.
Start small
  • Only add 5–10 accounts into this sequence at a time to keep quality high.

1.6 Referral & Partner Sequence

Goal
Turn your happy clients and partners into a steady stream of warm intros.
A. When to use this
  • You have clients, partners, or investors who like you but don’t send intros consistently.
B. Sequence structure
  • Day 1 – Email 1: “Could you introduce me to 2–3 companies like [X]?” with 3–4 clear ICP bullets.
  • Day 5–7 – Email 2: Soft reminder: “Still open to intros? Here’s a 2-sentence blurb you can forward.”
  • Day 14 – Email 3: Simple “no pressure, but here’s a case study you can share if it’s helpful.”

Optionally:

  • Task: DM on LinkedIn for top partners.
C. Fields in HirePilot
  • Partners/Referrers table or tags on Companies/Contacts.
  • Fields: Partner Type (VC, Agency, Client), Relationship Stage, Last Referral Date.
D. Build in HirePilot
Create a Campaign
  • Name: “Referral Engine – Q1 2026”.
  • Audience: Contacts tagged Partner = Yes.
Add steps
  • 2–3 emails as above.
  • Optional tasks for high-value partners.
Track outcomes
  • For each referral that becomes a lead, set Source = “Referral – [Partner]”.
  • Your dashboards can later show revenue by partner.

1.7 How to install sequences in HirePilot (for first-time users)

If a user has never done this before, the “minimum viable” steps are:

  1. Pick one sequence (e.g., Funding Signal).
  2. Create the Campaign in HirePilot and add the steps (emails + tasks) exactly as listed.
  3. Import or tag 20–50 contacts that fit the trigger.
  4. Turn on the sequence at low volume (10–15/day) and watch replies/meetings.
  5. Only after they’re comfortable, move on to Event Bridge, Inbound Intelligence, etc.

2.1 What workflows are (in plain English)

A workflow is a way to connect tools so data flows between them automatically. Instead of manually copying leads from RB2B to HirePilot, Zapier/Make does it for you.

Example: RB2B sees a company visit your pricing page → Zapier creates a Lead in HirePilot → HirePilot adds them to the “Inbound Intelligence” sequence → you get a Slack notification to review.

In this section, every workflow includes exact Zapier steps for first-timers, plus a manual alternative if you’re not ready for automation yet.

2.2 RB2B → HirePilot → Outreach workflow (your example, fully productized)

Goal: Every RB2B-identified visitor matching your ICP becomes a Lead in HirePilot and gets sequenced automatically.

A. What this workflow does

Overview
  • RB2B identifies a company visiting your site (pricing, GTM guide, demo).
  • Zapier creates a Company and Lead in HirePilot.
  • HirePilot auto-enriches (Apollo/Skrapp), tags intent, and enrolls in “Inbound Intelligence” sequence.
  • You get Slack notification only for high-intent leads.

B. Tools you need

Prereqs
  • RB2B account (already set up).
  • HirePilot account.
  • Zapier (free tier works for 100 tasks/month).
  • Apollo or Skrapp (for enrichment).

C. Exact Zapier steps (no experience needed)

Click-by-click
Zap 1: RB2B → HirePilot Lead creation
  1. 1 Go to zapier.com → “Create Zap”.
  2. 2 Trigger: RB2B → “New Company Identified”.
  3. 3 Connect your RB2B account.
  4. 4 Test trigger (RB2B sends recent visitor data).
  5. 5 Action 1: HirePilot → “Create Lead”.
  6. 6 Connect HirePilot account.
  7. 7 Map fields:
Field Mapping
  • Company Name = {{RB2B Company Name}}
  • Website = {{RB2B Website}}
  • Page Category = {{RB2B Page Visited}} (pricing, GTM guide, etc.)
  • Source = “RB2B Inbound”
  • Visit Intensity = {{RB2B Pageviews or Score}}
  • Persona = “RB2B – [Page Category]” (you’ll create this Persona)
  1. 8 Action 2: HirePilot → “Add to Campaign”.
  2. 9 Campaign = “Inbound Intelligence”. Lead = {{Lead created in previous step}}.
  3. 10 Action 3: Slack → “Send Channel Message” (optional, for review).
Slack details (optional)
Channel: #inbound-leads
Message: “New RB2B lead: {{Company Name}} visited {{Page Category}} | Lead ID: {{HirePilot Lead ID}}”

Turn on Zap → test with a manual RB2B trigger.

Zap 2: Auto-enrich (Apollo)
  • Trigger: HirePilot → “New Lead”.
  • Action: Apollo → “Enrich Contact”.
  • Domain = {{HirePilot Website}}.
  • Action: HirePilot → “Update Lead”.
  • Lead ID = {{Original Lead ID}}.
  • Map Apollo fields: First Name, Last Name, Email, Title, LinkedIn URL.

D. Manual alternative (no Zapier)

  • Check RB2B Slack channel daily.
  • Copy good-fit companies to CSV.
  • Import to HirePilot Leads → manually add to “Inbound Intelligence” campaign.

E. Safety rules built-in

  • Only trigger for ICP pages (pricing, GTM guide, demo, careers).
  • RB2B already filters low-quality traffic.
  • HirePilot dedupe prevents duplicates.
  • Start with 5–10 leads/day max.

2.3 Apollo list → HirePilot campaign workflow (always-on list rotation)

Goal: Fresh Apollo lists flow into HirePilot campaigns weekly without manual CSV work.

A. What this does

  • Apollo runs your Persona search (e.g., “Seed–Series A CEO”).
  • Apollo exports new leads matching recency/dedupe rules.
  • Zapier creates Leads in HirePilot → adds to correct Campaign.

B. Exact Zapier steps

Zap: Apollo → HirePilot
  • Trigger: Apollo → “New Contact” (or Schedule by Zapier → weekly).
  • Filter: Only continue if:
  • Title matches Persona keywords.
  • Company size 10–250.
  • Not contacted in last 60 days (Apollo has recency filter).
  • Action: HirePilot → “Create Lead”.
  • Map: Name, Email, Company, Title, LinkedIn, Source = “Apollo – [Persona]”.
  • Action: HirePilot → “Add to Campaign”.
  • Campaign = “Funding Signal” or Persona-specific campaign.
  • Action: Slack → “{{X}} new Apollo leads added to {{Campaign}}”.

C. Manual alternative

Apollo → Export → CSV → HirePilot Leads → add to Campaign. Do once/week.

2.4 Stripe → HirePilot revenue attribution workflow

Goal: Every payment automatically updates your pipeline dashboards with revenue data.

A. What this does

  • Stripe payment succeeds.
  • Zapier finds matching Opportunity in HirePilot (by email or metadata).
  • Updates Opportunity: Amount, Status = “Won”, Close Date, Product.

B. Exact Zapier steps

Zap: Stripe → HirePilot
  • Trigger: Stripe → “New Payment”.
  • Filter: Payment status = succeeded AND Amount > $X (your minimum).
  • Action: HirePilot → “Find Lead” or “Find Opportunity”. Search by Email = {{Stripe Customer Email}}.
  • Action: HirePilot → “Update Opportunity”.
  • Opportunity ID = {{Found Opportunity}}
  • Amount = {{Stripe Amount}}
  • Status = “Closed Won”
  • Close Date = {{Today}}
  • Product = {{Stripe Product}}
  • Action: Slack → “New revenue: ${{Amount}} from {{Customer Name}}”.

C. Manual alternative

Stripe dashboard → copy payment details → update HirePilot Opportunities manually.

2.5 Calendly → HirePilot meetings, no-shows, and follow-up workflow

Goal: Every meeting booked, attended, or no-showed automatically updates your pipeline and creates follow-ups.

A. What this does

  • Calendly meeting booked → create Opportunity in HirePilot.
  • Meeting marked “no-show” → create Task to reschedule.
  • Meeting completed → move Opportunity to “Discovery Done”.

B. Exact Zapier steps

Zap 1: New Calendly → HirePilot Opportunity
  • Trigger: Calendly → “Invitee Created”.
  • Action: HirePilot → “Create Opportunity”.
  • Name = “Meeting: {{Invitee Name}} – {{Event Type}}”
  • Lead = Find by Email ({{Invitee Email}})
  • Stage = “Meeting Booked”
  • Meeting Date = {{Event Start}}
Zap 2: No-show → Follow-up Task
  • Trigger: Calendly → “Invitee No-Showed”.
  • Action: HirePilot → “Create Task”.
  • Lead/Opportunity = {{From Zap 1}}
  • Task Type = “Reschedule”
  • Due Date = {{Event Date + 2 days}}

C. Manual alternative

Calendly → copy details → create Opportunity/Task in HirePilot manually.

2.6 Reactivation workflow (waking up old but good-fit leads)

Goal: Leads that went cold but are still ICP-fit get a short “re-engage” sequence.

A. What this does

  • HirePilot finds leads: No activity 90+ days, good Persona fit, no hard “do not contact”.
  • Adds them to “Reactivation” campaign (3 light touches).

B. Exact Zapier steps

Zap: HirePilot → Reactivation Campaign
  • Trigger: Schedule by Zapier → “Every Week” (Sunday night).
  • Action: HirePilot → “Find Records”.
  • Table: Leads.
  • Filters: Last Activity > 90 days ago, Persona = ICP, Do Not Contact = false.
  • Action: HirePilot → “Add to Campaign”.
  • Campaign: “Reactivation – {{Month}}”.

C. Manual alternative

  • HirePilot saved view: “Leads to Reactivate”.
  • Manually add 20–50/week to Reactivation campaign.

2.7 How to build workflows if you’re new to Zapier

For absolute beginners:

  1. 1Sign up at zapier.com (free tier = 100 tasks/month).
  2. 2Click “Create Zap” → search for first app (RB2B).
  3. 3Zapier will walk you through “Connect Account” → “Test Trigger”.
  4. 4Add Action apps one-by-one (HirePilot, Slack).
  5. 5Map fields using dropdowns ({{Field Name}}).
  6. 6Test end-to-end with real data.
  7. 7Turn on → monitor first 10 runs.

Pro tip: Start with RB2B → HirePilot Zap (highest ROI). Add others one-per-week.

Section 3

Dashboards that make decisions obvious

Dashboards aren’t “nice to have.” They’re what keeps your GTM system honest. If you can’t tell what’s working in under 30 seconds, you’re still operating on vibes.

Executive Overview
Runway, revenue, margin — the founder view.
Pipeline Pacing
Are we on track for meetings + proposals this month?
Channel Performance
What’s working by channel/persona — where to double down.

3.1 What makes a “good” GTM dashboard (and common mistakes)

A good GTM dashboard answers exactly three questions in under 30 seconds:

Are we on track?
(meetings, pipeline, revenue)
What’s working?
(channels, personas, campaigns)
What do we do tomorrow?
(next best actions)

Common mistakes to avoid

Guardrails
  • Too many widgets (max 6–8 per dashboard).
  • No targets (e.g., “meetings this week” without “vs goal”).
  • Stale data (dashboards must update live from HirePilot tables).
  • No slicing (can’t drill into “what’s working for Series A CEOs?”).

In HirePilot, dashboards pull from Tables (custom data) and core objects (Leads, Opportunities, Campaigns) automatically.

3.2 Executive Overview dashboard

Purpose: “Do I have enough runway? Are we profitable?” — for you as founder or exec team.

A. What it shows

  • Total pipeline value (YTD).
  • Closed revenue (YTD, by month).
  • Gross costs (events, tools, contractors).
  • Gross profit + margin.
  • “Break-even month” indicator.

B. HirePilot tables needed

  • Opportunities (Amount, Close Date, Stage, Status, Source, Product, Cost).
  • Events (Revenue, Gross Cost, Date).
  • Costs table (optional): Contractor hours, ad spend, tool costs.

C. Exact steps to build in HirePilot

Recipe
  1. 1 Go to Dashboards → New Dashboard → Name: “Executive Overview”.
Widget 1: Pipeline Value (YTD)
Data source: Opportunities
Metric: Sum of Amount
Filter: Close Date = this year
Display: Big number + sparkline
Widget 2: Revenue by Month
Data source: Opportunities
Metric: Sum of Amount
Group by: Close Date (Month)
Display: Bar chart
Widget 3: Gross Margin
Data source: Opportunities
Metric: (Sum Amount – Sum Cost) / Sum Amount
Filter: Status = Closed Won
Display: Big number + trend arrow
Widget 4: Break-even Month
Data source: Opportunities + Costs table
Metric: Running total Gross Profit
Filter: Cumulative profit > target monthly burn
Display: Text or milestone
Widget 5: Pipeline by Source
Data source: Opportunities
Metric: Count of Opportunities
Group by: Source
Filter: Stage != Closed Lost
Display: Donut chart

Set to update every 15 minutes. Share with exec team.

3.3 Pipeline & Meeting Pacing dashboard

Purpose: “Are we on track for meetings and proposals this month?” — daily check for your team.

A. What it shows

  • New leads created (weekly vs target).
  • Meetings booked (this week vs target).
  • Proposals sent (this month vs target).
  • Pipeline velocity (leads → meetings → proposals).
  • “Meetings remaining needed” to hit goal.

B. HirePilot tables needed

  • Leads (Created Date, Source, Persona, Campaign).
  • Tasks (Type = Meeting, Status).
  • Opportunities (Stage, Amount, Created Date).

C. Exact steps to build

Recipe
  1. 1 New Dashboard → Name: “Pipeline Pacing”.
Widget 1: New Leads This Week
Data source: Leads
Metric: Count
Filter: Created Date = this week
Target line: 200 (adjust to your goal)
Display: Big number + vs target %
Widget 2: Meetings Booked (Week)
Data source: Tasks
Filter: Type = “Meeting”, Status = Booked/Completed, Due Date = this week
Metric: Count
Target: 15–25
Display: Big number + trend
Widget 3: Pipeline Funnel
Data source: Leads + Opportunities
Stages: New Lead → Replied → Meeting → Proposal → Won
Display: Funnel chart
Widget 4: Meetings Needed
Formula: (Monthly revenue goal / Avg deal size / Close rate) – Meetings booked
Display: Big warning number if behind
Widget 5: Top Campaigns
Data source: Leads
Metric: Meetings generated

Section 4

Automation Map (what to automate vs keep human)

This section gives you the “automation boundary” for your HirePilot GTM operating system: what the system should do automatically vs where humans must stay in the loop.

4.1 Principle 4.2 Automate-first 4.3 Human-first 4.4 Guardrails 4.5 Blueprint 4.6 Quarterly review

4.1 The “80–90% automated” principle (and why not 100%)

Your GTM operating system should handle repetitive, rules-based work so your team can focus on conversations, judgment, and relationships.

Why 80–90%, not 100%?
  • Automation excels at scale and consistency (sending 100 emails, creating tasks, enriching contacts).
  • Humans excel at nuance, personalization, and reading between the lines (handling objections, crafting a pitch for a specific exec).
The goal

Spend 10–20% of GTM time on “system work” and 80–90% on revenue conversations.

Practical meaning: if your team is stuck doing manual admin, your system isn’t finished yet.

4.2 Automate-first list: tasks the system should always do for you

These are non-negotiable automations. Once set up, they should run without thinking.

Task Why automate HirePilot feature + integrations
List pulls (Apollo, RB2B, partner lists) No one should manually export CSVs weekly Apollo → HirePilot Leads, RB2B → HirePilot via Zapier
Sequence scheduling (email + LinkedIn timing) Humans forget follow-ups; consistency wins HirePilot Campaign Scheduler + send windows
Task creation (from replies, no-shows, high-intent) Reps should only see “next best action” HirePilot Workflows (reply → call task)

The punchline: if it’s repetitive, rules-based, and happens every week, the system should do it.

4.6 How to review and adjust your automation map every quarter

Quarterly automation audit (30 minutes):

  1. 1 Check dashboards: Which channels/campaigns have best ROI? Double automation there.
  2. 2 Review task lists: Are reps doing repetitive work? Automate it.
  3. 3 Deliverability report: Any campaigns consistently >3% bounce or low opens? Pause + diagnose.
  4. 4 New tool evaluation: Worth adding (e.g., new enrichment tool, AI copy)? Test → measure → decide.
  5. 5 Team feedback: “What’s still manual that feels stupid?” Fix it.
  6. 6 Update your Automation Map doc with what changed. Share with team.
Automation maturity levels
  • Level 1: Manual CSVs → HirePilot (start here)
  • Level 2: Basic sequences + Zapier for 1–2 tools
  • Level 3: Full workflows + live dashboards (this playbook)
  • Level 4: Custom tables + REX Chat insights + advanced ABM
Most teams win at Level 3. Don’t optimize past what moves the revenue needle.
